## Acid-Base Titration: Finding Molarity
**Subject:** chemistry
**Level:** ExperimentLevel.BEGINNER
**Duration:** 45 minutes

### Learning Objectives
- Understand acid-base neutralization reactions
- Learn proper titration technique
- Determine unknown solution concentration
- Read meniscus correctly
- Calculate molarity from experimental data

### Materials Required
- Unknown acid solution (~0.1 M HCl)
- Standard NaOH solution (0.1 M)
- Burette
- Erlenmeyer flask
- Pipette
- Phenolphthalein indicator
- Distilled water
- Beaker

### Steps (4 total)
1. **Setup and Calibration**
   Prepare apparatus and standardize burette

2. **Add Acid and Indicator**
   Prepare flask with unknown acid and indicator

3. **Titration Process**
   Slowly add base until endpoint is reached

4. **Calculate Results**
   Use titration data to find acid molarity

## Hooke's Law: Spring Constant Determination
**Subject:** physics
**Level:** ExperimentLevel.BEGINNER
**Duration:** 45 minutes

- Determine spring constant experimentally
- Create force vs displacement graphs
- Understand linear relationships in physics
- Apply mathematical modeling to physical systems

### Materials Required
- Spring
- Retort stand
- Clamp
- Ruler or measuring tape (0-50 cm)
- Various masses (100g, 200g, 300g, 400g, 500g)
- Mass hanger
- Paper and pencil

### Steps (4 total)
1. **Setup the Apparatus**
   Mount spring vertically on retort stand

2. **Add Masses and Measure**
   Add masses incrementally and record displacement

3. **Calculate Spring Constant**
   Use F = kx to find spring constant

4. **Plot Results**
   Create force vs displacement graph
